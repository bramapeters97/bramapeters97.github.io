Lost In Translation

Living in beautiful Stockholm, I sometimes missed my Dutch stroopwafels and stamppot — especially while staring at the bare 'Scandinavian-minimalist' walls of my condo. What started as a joke (and a way to practice Swedish) turned into the Stokhollum Spoorkaart: a Dutchified metro map with translations so literal, even Google Translate blushed. I just thought it was funny. Strangely, friends and family loved it and wanted one for their city. So I built a clever (and very secret) algorithm — and now anyone can create their own absurd, surreal, and gloriously inaccurate metro map for their hometown. Global Transit, Local Nonsense. Just mind the (language) gap.
